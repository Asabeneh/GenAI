## Getting Started

### Q: Do I need programming skills to use AI tools?

**A: No!** Modern AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are designed for everyone. You just type what you want in normal language (English, Spanish, etc.) and the AI responds. Think of it like using Google or sending a text message‚Äîno coding required.

---

### Q: Which AI tool should I start with?

**A: ChatGPT** is the most beginner-friendly for several reasons:
- Free tier with generous limits
- Large community (easy to find help)
- Well-documented
- Wide range of capabilities

Once comfortable, try Claude (great for analysis) or Gemini (integrates with Google services).

---

### Q: How long does it take to learn?

**A:** 
- **Basic usage:** 15-30 minutes to start using
- **Confident user:** 1-2 weeks of daily practice
- **Proficient user:** 1-2 months of regular use
- **Expert prompt engineer:** 3-6 months of dedicated practice

Good news: You can get value immediately, even as a beginner!

---

### Q: Is there an age requirement?

**A:** Most AI tools require users to be:
- **13+ years old** for basic accounts (with parental consent)
- **18+ years old** for accounts without parental consent

Check specific terms of service for each platform.

## Using AI Tools

### Q: How do I write a good prompt?

**A:** Follow the "Context + Request + Constraints" formula:

**Example:**
```
Context: "I'm a teacher creating a lesson plan for 5th graders"
Request: "Write a 10-minute activity about the water cycle"
Constraints: "Make it hands-on and require only common household items"
```

See our [prompt engineering guide](./prompt.md) for more tips!

---

### Q: Why did AI give me a bad response?

**A:** Common reasons:
1. **Prompt too vague:** "Tell me about marketing" ‚Üí "Write a 200-word guide to social media marketing for small businesses"
2. **Lack of context:** "Write an email" ‚Üí "Write a professional email to a client apologizing for a delayed shipment"
3. **Unrealistic expectations:** AI isn't perfect‚Äîit needs good inputs to give good outputs

**Solution:** Refine your prompt and try again! AI gets better with iteration.

---

### Q: Can I continue a previous conversation?

**A:** Depends on the tool:
- **ChatGPT:** Yes, keeps conversation history (you can also start new chats)
- **Claude:** Yes, maintains conversation context
- **Gemini:** Yes, with conversation history

**Tip:** Start a new conversation when switching to a completely different topic.

---

### Q: How do I make AI stop generating?

**A:** 
- **ChatGPT:** Click the "Stop generating" button that appears while generating
- **Claude:** Click "Stop" or close the response
- **Gemini:** Click "Stop generating"

You can then ask AI to continue or revise what it started.

---

### Q: Can I use AI in languages other than English?

**A: Yes!** Major AI tools support 50+ languages including:
- Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese
- Chinese (Mandarin), Japanese, Korean
- Arabic, Hindi, Russian
- And many more

**Quality varies:** English generally works best because models were trained more on English text, but other major languages work very well too.

## Costs and Pricing

### Q: Are AI tools free?

**A:** Most have free tiers:

| Tool | Free Tier | Paid Tier | Cost |
|------|-----------|-----------|------|
| **ChatGPT** | ‚úÖ GPT-3.5 | GPT-4, DALL-E, priority access | $20/month |
| **Claude** | ‚úÖ Limited messages | More messages, priority | $20/month |
| **Gemini** | ‚úÖ Basic model | Advanced model, more features | $20/month |
| **Copilot** | ‚úÖ With limits | More quota | Varies |

**For learning:** Free tiers are more than enough!

---

### Q: Is the paid version worth it?

**A:** Upgrade if you:
- Use AI heavily every day (running into free tier limits)
- Need the latest/most capable models (GPT-4 vs GPT-3.5)
- Require faster response times
- Use for professional work where quality is critical

**Start free first** and upgrade only when you feel limited.

---

### Q: Are there hidden costs?

**A:** No hidden costs for chat-based tools like ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. However:
- Some specialized AI tools (image generation, voice cloning) may charge per use
- API access (for developers) charges by tokens used
- Some features require paid tiers

**Bottom line:** For basic learning and use, free tiers have no hidden costs.

## Privacy and Security

### Q: Is my data safe with AI tools?

**A:** Generally yes, but understand:

**What AI companies collect:**
- Your prompts and conversations
- Account information (email, name)
- Usage data (when you use it, how often)

**What they say they do:**
- Use data to improve AI (with opt-out options in some cases)
- Not sell your personal data to third parties
- Store conversations securely

**Always read privacy policies** for specific tools you use.

---

### Q: Can AI "remember" personal information about me?

**A:** 
- **Within a conversation:** Yes, AI remembers what you told it earlier
- **Across conversations:** Usually no (each new chat starts fresh)
- **Exception:** Some tools now offer "memory" features you can enable

**Important:** Don't share:
- Passwords
- Credit card numbers
- Social security numbers
- Private health information
- Confidential company information

---

### Q: Who can see my AI conversations?

**A:**
- **Generally:** Only you can see your conversations
- **Company staff:** May review conversations to improve AI or for safety reasons
- **Exceptions:** If you share a conversation link or screenshot

**Best practice:** Treat AI conversations like you would email‚Äîsomewhat private but not completely confidential.

---

### Q: Can I delete my conversation history?

**A: Yes!** Most platforms allow you to:
- Delete individual conversations
- Clear all history
- Disable conversation saving

**Check settings** in your AI tool account.

## Capabilities and Limitations

### Q: What can AI actually do?

**A:** AI excels at:

‚úÖ **Writing and communication**
- Emails, reports, articles, creative writing
- Summarizing long texts
- Translating languages

‚úÖ **Learning and education**
- Explaining complex concepts simply
- Creating study materials
- Answering questions
- Providing step-by-step tutorials

‚úÖ **Creative work**
- Brainstorming ideas
- Generating images (with image AI)
- Writing code
- Creating content variations

‚úÖ **Productivity**
- Planning and scheduling
- Organizing information
- Data analysis assistance
- Task automation ideas

---

### Q: What can't AI do (or do poorly)?

**A:** AI struggles with:

‚ùå **Real-time information**
- Current news (unless web-enabled)
- Today's weather
- Live stock prices
- Recent events after its training cutoff

‚ùå **Personal information**
- Facts about you (unless you tell it)
- Your calendar or email
- Your specific situation details

‚ùå **High-stakes decisions**
- Medical diagnoses
- Legal advice
- Financial investment decisions
- Relationship advice

‚ùå **100% accuracy**
- Can make mistakes
- Can "hallucinate" (make up facts)
- Math errors possible

‚ùå **Accessing external systems**
- Can't browse websites (unless specifically enabled)
- Can't send emails for you
- Can't execute code on your computer

---

### Q: Can AI access the internet?

**A:** Depends on the tool:
- **ChatGPT Free:** No web access
- **ChatGPT Plus (with browsing):** Yes, when enabled
- **Copilot:** Yes, includes web search
- **Gemini:** Yes, can search web
- **Perplexity AI:** Yes, designed for web-based answers

**Note:** Even with web access, AI might not give real-time results.

---

### Q: Is AI always right?

**A: No!** AI can:
- Make mistakes in calculations
- "Hallucinate" (confidently state false information)
- Provide outdated information
- Misunderstand your question

**Always verify:**
- Important facts and statistics
- Medical, legal, or financial advice
- Critical business information
- Academic research

**Rule of thumb:** Use AI as a first draft or research starting point, not the final authority.

---

### Q: Can AI see images I upload?

**A:** Depends on the model:
- **GPT-4V (ChatGPT Plus):** Yes, can analyze images
- **Claude (some versions):** Yes, can analyze images
- **Gemini:** Yes, can analyze images
- **GPT-3.5 (ChatGPT Free):** No image analysis

**What it can do with images:**
- Describe what's in the image
- Extract text (OCR)
- Answer questions about the image
- Provide analysis or suggestions

## Ethics and Best Practices

### Q: Is using AI "cheating"?

**A:** It depends on context:

**Acceptable use:**
‚úÖ Using AI to brainstorm ideas, then developing them yourself
‚úÖ Using AI to improve your draft writing
‚úÖ Using AI to learn concepts, then applying knowledge yourself
‚úÖ Using AI as a tool (like spell-check or calculator)

**Problematic use:**
‚ùå Submitting AI work as entirely your own without disclosure
‚ùå Using AI to complete assignments meant to test your understanding
‚ùå Using AI to avoid learning fundamental skills
‚ùå Violating academic integrity policies

**Best practice:** 
- Disclose when you've used AI assistance (especially for school/work)
- Use AI to enhance your work, not replace your thinking
- Check your institution's/employer's AI usage policy

---

### Q: Do I need to cite or credit AI?

**A:** Best practices:

**For academic work:**
- **Yes, disclose AI use** (exact format varies by institution)
- Example: "Draft assisted by ChatGPT, edited and verified by author"
- Follow your school's citation guidelines

**For professional work:**
- Disclose to stakeholders if relevant
- Company policies vary‚Äîcheck yours
- Be transparent about process

**For personal use:**
- Generally no need to cite
- But don't claim AI's creative work as entirely original

---

### Q: Can I copyright AI-generated content?

**A:** Complex and evolving area:

**Current general guidance:**
- **Purely AI-generated content:** May not be copyrightable
- **Human-edited AI content:** Likely copyrightable (the human edits)
- **AI as a tool:** If you substantially modify AI output, likely copyrightable

**Best approach:**
- Use AI as a tool to assist your creative process
- Add significant human input and editing
- Consult a lawyer for important commercial work
- Laws are still developing‚Äîthis area is changing

---

### Q: How can I use AI responsibly?

**A:** Follow these principles:

‚úÖ **Transparency:** Disclose AI use when relevant
‚úÖ **Verification:** Check important facts and claims
‚úÖ **Privacy:** Don't share others' private information with AI
‚úÖ **Fairness:** Be aware of potential biases in AI outputs
‚úÖ **Learning:** Use AI to enhance skills, not avoid developing them
‚úÖ **Respect:** Follow your organization's AI policies
‚úÖ **Critical thinking:** Don't blindly accept AI outputs

## Technical Questions

### Q: What's the difference between GPT-3.5 and GPT-4?

**A:**

**GPT-3.5 (Free ChatGPT):**
- Faster responses
- Good for most tasks
- Less sophisticated reasoning
- No image analysis

**GPT-4 (ChatGPT Plus):**
- More advanced reasoning
- Better at complex tasks
- Can analyze images
- More creative
- More accurate
- Slower and costs $20/month

**For learning:** GPT-3.5 is fine! Upgrade to GPT-4 when you need advanced capabilities.

---

### Q: What are "tokens" and why do they matter?

**A:**
- **Token:** A chunk of text (roughly 3/4 of a word)
- **Example:** "Artificial Intelligence" ‚âà 3-4 tokens

**Why they matter:**
- AI has token limits (how much it can process at once)
- Free tiers may limit tokens per month
- Affects conversation length and document size

**Rough guide:**
- 100 tokens ‚âà 75 words
- 1,000 tokens ‚âà 750 words
- ChatGPT-3.5: 4,096 token limit per conversation
- ChatGPT-4: 8,192 or 32,768 token limit (depending on model)

---

### Q: What's "context window"?

**A:** The amount of text AI can "remember" or consider at once.

**Example:**
- Small context: Can only consider the last few messages
- Large context: Can consider an entire conversation or long document

**Practical impact:**
- Larger context = better understanding of long conversations
- Larger context = can work with longer documents
- GPT-4 has a larger context window than GPT-3.5

---

### Q: Can I run AI on my own computer?

**A:** 
- **Chat AI (like ChatGPT):** Generally no, too resource-intensive
- **Smaller AI models:** Yes, possible with technical setup
- **Local image AI:** Yes, tools like Stable Diffusion can run locally

**For most users:** Cloud-based tools (ChatGPT, Claude) are easier and more powerful.

## Troubleshooting

### Q: AI says it can't do something I know it can do. Why?

**A:** This happens because:
1. **Overly cautious:** AI errs on the side of caution
2. **Misunderstood request:** Rephrase your prompt
3. **Policy restrictions:** Some topics have restrictions

**Solution:** Try rephrasing:
```
Instead of: "Write my essay"
Try: "Help me outline an essay about [topic] with main points and supporting arguments"
```

---

### Q: AI's response was cut off mid-sentence. What happened?

**A:** Probably hit the response length limit.

**Solutions:**
- Ask AI to "continue" or "keep going"
- Ask for the complete section: "Finish the last paragraph"
- Request shorter responses upfront: "Keep under 300 words"

---

### Q: AI keeps giving me the same type of response. How do I get variety?

**A:** Try:
1. **Ask for alternatives:** "Give me 3 different versions"
2. **Specify style:** "Make it more casual" or "Make it more formal"
3. **Add constraints:** "Use a metaphor" or "Include a personal story"
4. **Start a new conversation:** Fresh start can help

---

### Q: I get an error message. What should I do?

**A:** Common fixes:

**"Too many requests" error:**
- Wait a few minutes and try again
- Free tiers have rate limits

**"Network error":**
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page
- Wait and retry

**"Service unavailable":**
- Servers might be down
- Check status page (openai.com/status for ChatGPT)
- Try again later

**Login issues:**
- Clear browser cache/cookies
- Try incognito/private window
- Reset password if needed

---

### Q: AI response is in the wrong language. How do I fix it?

**A:** 
```
Simply say: "Please respond in English" (or your preferred language)
```

Or be explicit in your prompt:
```
"Explain [topic] in simple Spanish for beginners"
```

---

### Q: How do I save/export my conversation?

**A:**
- **ChatGPT:** Click on your chat, then click share icon ‚Üí Copy link, or export data in settings
- **Claude:** Copy and paste conversation into a document
- **Gemini:** Share or export options in menu

**Best practice:** Copy important responses to a document‚Äîdon't rely solely on AI platforms to save them.
