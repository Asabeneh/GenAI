**A Beginner-Friendly Guide to AI Terms**

This glossary explains common terms you'll encounter when working with Generative AI. Terms are organized alphabetically with simple explanations and real-world examples.

---

## A

### AI (Artificial Intelligence)
**Simple Definition:** Computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence.

**Example:** When your phone recognizes your face to unlock, that's AI. When Spotify recommends songs you might like, that's also AI.

**Related Terms:** Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Generative AI

### API (Application Programming Interface)
**Simple Definition:** A way for different software applications to talk to each other.

**Example:** When you use a weather app on your phone, it uses an API to get weather data from a weather service.

**Why it matters:** Many AI tools offer APIs so developers can integrate AI into their own applications.

### Algorithm
**Simple Definition:** A set of step-by-step instructions for solving a problem or completing a task.

**Example:** A recipe is an algorithm for cooking. Google's search algorithm decides which websites to show you first.

**In AI context:** AI uses algorithms to learn from data and make predictions or create content.

---

## B

### Bias
**Simple Definition:** When an AI system shows unfair preferences based on its training data.

**Example:** If an AI is trained mostly on photos of light-skinned people, it might not recognize dark-skinned faces as accurately.

**Why it matters:** We need to be aware of bias in AI and work to reduce it.

### Bot / Chatbot
**Simple Definition:** A program that can have conversations with people, often to answer questions or provide assistance.

**Example:** The chat widget on a company's website that answers customer questions is often a chatbot.

**Related Terms:** Virtual Assistant, Conversational AI

---

## C

### ChatGPT
**Simple Definition:** A popular conversational AI tool created by OpenAI that can chat with you, answer questions, write content, and more.

**Example:** You can ask ChatGPT "Explain quantum physics simply" and it will give you an understandable explanation.

**Why it's important:** It's one of the most widely-used and beginner-friendly AI tools.

### Claude
**Simple Definition:** An AI assistant created by Anthropic, similar to ChatGPT, known for being helpful, harmless, and honest.

**Example:** Claude can help you write emails, analyze documents, or brainstorm ideas.

**When to use:** Great for longer documents and thoughtful analysis.

### Context
**Simple Definition:** The background information that helps AI understand what you're asking for.

**Example:**
- Without context: "Write about dogs"
- With context: "I'm writing a blog post for new dog owners about house training puppies. Write about dogs."

**Why it matters:** Providing good context leads to much better AI responses.

### Content Generation
**Simple Definition:** Using AI to create new content like text, images, music, or videos.

**Example:** Using DALL-E to create an image of "a cat wearing a spacesuit on Mars" is content generation.

---

## D

### DALL-E
**Simple Definition:** An AI tool by OpenAI that creates images from text descriptions.

**Example:** You type "a photo of a cozy coffee shop on a rainy day" and DALL-E generates an image matching that description.

**Pronunciation:** "DOLL-E" (like the toy doll + the letter E)

### Dataset
**Simple Definition:** A collection of data used to train an AI model.

**Example:** To teach AI to recognize cats, you might use a dataset of 10,000 cat photos.

**Why it matters:** The quality and diversity of datasets affect how well AI performs.

### Deep Learning
**Simple Definition:** A type of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers to learn from data.

**Example:** Deep learning powers facial recognition, language translation, and self-driving cars.

**Think of it as:** AI that learns in deep, complex ways similar to how human brains process information.

### Diffusion Model
**Simple Definition:** A type of AI model that creates images by gradually removing "noise" from random patterns.

**Example:** Stable Diffusion and DALL-E use diffusion models to generate images.

**Analogy:** Like sculpting a statue by gradually revealing the image from a block of marble.

---

## E

### Embedding
**Simple Definition:** A way of converting words or concepts into numbers that AI can understand and process.

**Example:** The word "cat" might be converted to a series of numbers that represent its meaning.

**Why it matters:** This is how AI understands language and finds relationships between different concepts.

### Ethics (AI Ethics)
**Simple Definition:** The study of right and wrong ways to develop and use AI.

**Example:** Questions like "Should AI be allowed to make hiring decisions?" fall under AI ethics.

**Why it matters:** Using AI responsibly means thinking about its impact on people and society.

---

## F

### Fine-tuning
**Simple Definition:** Taking a general AI model and training it further on specific data to make it better at particular tasks.

**Example:** Taking a general language model and training it on medical texts to make it better at medical questions.

**Analogy:** Like a general athlete (good at many sports) doing extra training to excel at basketball specifically.

### Few-shot Learning
**Simple Definition:** A prompt technique where you give AI a few examples of what you want before asking it to do a similar task.

**Example:**
```
"Apple -> Fruit
Car -> Vehicle
Dog -> Animal
Piano -> [AI completes: Musical Instrument]"
```

---

## G

### Gemini
**Simple Definition:** Google's AI assistant (formerly called Bard) that can chat, search, answer questions, and create content.

**Example:** You can ask Gemini to summarize a long article or help you write code.

**Special feature:** Integrates well with Google services like Gmail and Google Docs.

### Generative AI
**Simple Definition:** AI that can create new content rather than just analyze existing content.

**Example:** ChatGPT generating a story, DALL-E creating an image, or an AI composing music.

**Contrast with:** Traditional AI that classifies or predicts (like spam filters or recommendation systems).

### GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer)
**Simple Definition:** The technology behind ChatGPT. A type of AI model that's pre-trained on massive amounts of text.

**Example:** GPT-4 is the model that powers the latest version of ChatGPT.

**What the name means:**
- **Generative:** It creates new content
- **Pre-trained:** Trained on lots of data before you use it
- **Transformer:** The type of AI architecture it uses

---

## H

### Hallucination
**Simple Definition:** When AI confidently states false information that sounds plausible.

**Example:** AI might invent fake statistics or made-up sources that seem real but don't exist.

**Why it happens:** AI predicts what sounds likely, not always what's true.

**What to do:** Always verify important facts, especially dates, statistics, and specific claims.

---

## I

### Input
**Simple Definition:** The information or instructions you give to an AI system.

**Example:** When you type a question into ChatGPT, that's your input.

**Related Terms:** Prompt, Query, Request

### Iteration
**Simple Definition:** The process of refining something through repeated improvements.

**Example:**
```
Try 1: "Write about AI" (vague)
Try 2: "Write about AI in healthcare" (better)
Try 3: "Write a 200-word explanation of how AI is used in medical diagnosis, for a general audience" (best)
```

---

## L

### Large Language Model (LLM)
**Simple Definition:** An AI model trained on huge amounts of text that can understand and generate human-like language.

**Examples:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, LLaMA

**"Large" means:** These models have billions of parameters (adjustable parts that help them learn).

**What they can do:** Write, translate, summarize, answer questions, generate code, and more.

### Latency
**Simple Definition:** The delay between when you ask AI something and when it starts responding.

**Example:** If AI takes 3 seconds to start generating an answer, that's 3 seconds of latency.

**Why it matters:** Lower latency means faster, more responsive AI.

---

## M

### Machine Learning
**Simple Definition:** A type of AI where computers learn from data without being explicitly programmed for every scenario.

**Example:** Instead of programming every rule for recognizing spam email, machine learning lets the computer learn patterns from examples of spam vs. non-spam.

**Analogy:** Like how you learned to ride a bike through practice, not by memorizing every possible scenario.

### Midjourney
**Simple Definition:** A popular AI tool for creating artistic images from text descriptions.

**Example:** Type "a majestic lion made of crystal in a mystical forest" and Midjourney creates an artistic image.

**Known for:** High-quality, artistic, and creative images.

### Model
**Simple Definition:** The AI "brain" that's been trained on data to perform tasks.

**Example:** GPT-4 is a model, DALL-E is a model, etc.

**Analogy:** Like a trained musician who learned by practicing for years. The model is the result of all that training.

---

## N

### Natural Language Processing (NLP)
**Simple Definition:** The field of AI focused on helping computers understand, interpret, and generate human language.

**Examples:** Chatbots, translation tools, voice assistants, sentiment analysis.

**Why it matters:** NLP is what allows you to chat with AI in normal English instead of computer code.

### Neural Network
**Simple Definition:** A type of AI modeled loosely on how human brains work, with interconnected "neurons" that process information.

**Example:** The AI behind facial recognition uses neural networks to identify faces.

**Why "neural":** Named because it's inspired by biological neurons (brain cells).

---

## O

### Output
**Simple Definition:** The response or result that AI generates based on your input.

**Example:** When ChatGPT writes an email for you, that email is the output.

**Related Terms:** Response, Generation, Result

### Overfitting
**Simple Definition:** When an AI model is too specifically trained on its training data and doesn't work well on new, different data.

**Example:** An AI trained only on photos of golden retrievers might think all dogs are golden retrievers.

**Why it's a problem:** The model memorized specifics instead of learning general patterns.

---

## P

### Parameter
**Simple Definition:** Adjustable values inside an AI model that get fine-tuned during training.

**Example:** GPT-3 has 175 billion parameters that work together to understand and generate language.

**Analogy:** Like knobs on a mixing board that get adjusted to create the perfect sound.

**Why "billions matter":** More parameters generally means the model can learn more complex patterns.

### Prompt
**Simple Definition:** The instruction or question you give to an AI to get it to generate a response.

**Example:** "Write a haiku about coffee" is a prompt.

**Quality matters:** Better prompts lead to better results.

**Related Terms:** Input, Query, Instruction

### Prompt Engineering
**Simple Definition:** The skill of crafting effective prompts to get the best results from AI.

**Example:**
- Basic: "Write about exercise"
- Engineered: "Write a motivational 150-word Instagram post about the benefits of morning exercise for busy professionals. Use an encouraging but realistic tone."

**Why it's important:** Good prompt engineering can dramatically improve AI outputs.

---

## R

### RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
**Simple Definition:** A technique where AI searches for relevant information before generating a response.

**Example:** Instead of just using its training data, the AI might search a database or the web first, then use that information to answer your question.

**Benefit:** More accurate, up-to-date, and source-based responses.

### Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF)
**Simple Definition:** A training method where AI improves by getting feedback from human reviewers on whether its responses are good or bad.

**Example:** Humans rate AI responses as helpful/unhelpful, and the AI learns to generate more helpful responses.

**Why it matters:** This is how AI assistants like ChatGPT learn to be more helpful and align with human values.

---

## S

### Stable Diffusion
**Simple Definition:** An open-source AI model for generating images from text descriptions.

**Example:** Similar to DALL-E but free and can run on your own computer.

**Known for:** Being accessible and customizable.

### Sentiment Analysis
**Simple Definition:** Using AI to determine the emotional tone of text (positive, negative, neutral).

**Example:** Analyzing customer reviews to see if people are happy or frustrated.

**Use cases:** Customer service, social media monitoring, market research.

---

## T

### Temperature
**Simple Definition:** A setting that controls how creative or random AI responses are.

**Low temperature (0.1-0.3):** More focused, consistent, predictable
**High temperature (0.7-1.0):** More creative, varied, random

**Example:**
- Low temp prompt: "What's the capital of France?" â†’ "Paris" (you want consistency)
- High temp prompt: "Write a creative story" â†’ More varied and creative outputs

### Token
**Simple Definition:** A piece of text (roughly 3/4 of a word) that AI processes.

**Example:** "Artificial Intelligence" = about 3-4 tokens

**Why it matters:** Many AI tools have token limits (e.g., can process 4,000 tokens at once).

**Rough conversion:** 100 tokens â‰ˆ 75 words

### Training
**Simple Definition:** The process of teaching an AI model by showing it lots of examples.

**Example:** Training an image AI by showing it millions of cat photos until it learns what cats look like.

**Time:** Can take weeks or months for large models.

**Cost:** Training large AI models can cost millions of dollars in computing power.

### Transformer
**Simple Definition:** A type of neural network architecture that's especially good at understanding relationships in data, particularly language.

**Example:** GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) uses this architecture.

**Why it's revolutionary:** Transformers can process entire sentences at once (unlike older models that processed word by word).

**Impact:** Enabled the current generation of powerful language AIs.

---

## V

### Vector Database
**Simple Definition:** A database specialized for storing and searching AI embeddings (numerical representations of data).

**Example:** Used to store and quickly search through millions of document embeddings.

**Why it matters:** Enables fast similarity search (find documents similar to this one).

---

## Z

### Zero-shot Learning
**Simple Definition:** When AI can perform a task it wasn't explicitly trained for, without any examples.

**Example:** Asking ChatGPT to translate to a language it saw little of in training, and it still does reasonably well.

**Contrast with:** Few-shot learning (giving examples) and fine-tuning (additional training).
